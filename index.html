THE STRAY PROBLEMS IN THE PHILIPPINES

According to a survey conducted by the Philippines Animal Welfare Society (PAWS), there were around 12 million stray dogs and cats in the country in 2019. These animals are often found wandering the streets, searching for food and shelter. Unfortunately, many of them are malnourished, sick, and injured.

the issue of stray animals in the Philippines is a complex one that requires a multi-faceted approach. It is important to raise awareness about the plight of these animals and to promote responsible pet ownership. It is also important to support organizations that are working to rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome stray animals. By working together, we can help to create a better future for these animals and for our communities.

YOU CAN HELP US BY

DONATING

By donating, you can help many stray animals and provide them with the necessary medical care, vaccinations, food, and bedding material they need to survive. You can also help control the population of stray animals by supporting spaying and neutering programs.


Additionally, you can educate yourself and others on responsible feeding of stray animals, which involves feeding them the right amount of food, out of sight from high traffic areas, and not taming them. Your kindness and compassion can make a difference in the lives of these animals.

ADOPTING

By adopting, you can help many stray animals and provide them with a loving home. Adopting a pet is a great way to give an animal a second chance at life and provide them with the care and attention they need.


Additionally, adopting a pet can help reduce the number of animals in shelters and on the streets. WEBSITE YOU CAN VISIT TO LEARN MORE

The Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS) is a non-government organization that aims to prevent animal cruelty through education, animal sheltering, and advocacy. It was founded in 1954 by Muriel Jay and is based in Quezon City, Philippines. PAWS has developed programs designed to promote humane treatment of all animals, including low-cost spay-neuter clinics, vaccination programs, and trap-neuter-return initiatives.

The organization also takes a firm stand on matters relating to animal welfare and continues to lobby for better laws to ensure humane treatment of all animals.
